Westward Expansion

How Western Land Should be Added to the Union

Julian Reed for The Lily

1I believe that the only possible way for our newly acquired land to be brought into the United States of America is as free land, which would set the groundwork for the abolition of slavery and the freedom for all in this country by exemplifying the possible success of a non-slavery state.

I predict that the economic effects of banning slavery in the Western land will be extremely beneficial to the entire country. Primarily, banning slavery allows for a free labor market in these new states, a market that every person in the US, white and black, male and female. If slavery were to be allowed, new plantations would surely form given California’s warm climate, bringing many thousands of slaves with them.2 This is far worse for the average American than the many jobs that would be created in this free land, as the majority of Americans wouldn’t get the chance for social mobility, while others would miss the opportunity to feed their families.

As Lydia Maria Child notes in her book The Frugal Housewife, women are still active parts of the labor market, doing things like sewing clothes and cooking meals for a wage.3 This means that for marginalized groups, like women and free blacks, this would create the opportunity to be incorporated as equal members of society and avoid a future of low wages and long hours.4

The competition created by this influx of labor would create a thriving economy in the West, which could then be used as a model for the rest of the country.

Making Western land free is also important from a religious perspective. Many people across the country are Christians, as shown by the “deep interest” blacks have in the Church and the “encouragement from all classes” that preachers receive.5-6 In fact, many southern plantation owners read certain Bible passages to their slaves, including the story of Noah, to reinforce the idea that slavery is good.7 However, the Bible can also be used to prove otherwise, specifically in the book of Exodus, which gives the slaves hope that they will be eventually freed.8

Since religion is already a large part of life in California, it is logical that religion will continue to spread throughout the West.9 If religion is prevalent, and an important guide for how people live their lives, having a unified Church and the unified people that come with it is critical to the success of the West. Therefore, slavery must be abolished in these areas, so that the Church and its ideas may stay unified, a unity that would contribute significantly to the success of the Western states.

If Western states can be successful without slavery, which I believe they can for the reasons explained above, it would be a very important step towards the abolition of slavery. These Western successes could then be applied in the South, continuing the long and difficult process of abolition, eventually resulting in the freedom of African Americans. At that point, the rights and liberties of all groups would be reexamined, and many groups would be given full rights, finally establishing the great American goal of all being created equal.10
